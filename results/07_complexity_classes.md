# 07 Complexity Classes

## Purpose

This notebook establishes the working computational vocabulary for interpreting arXiv:2606.14806. The key issue is whether semiclassical gravity grants efficient NP-complete problem-solving power through nonlinear dynamics.

## Complexity classes

- **P** — Problems efficiently solvable by deterministic classical computation. _Role:_ Observed classical baseline.
- **NP** — Problems whose proposed solutions can be efficiently verified. _Role:_ Contains NP-complete problems.
- **NP-complete** — The hardest problems in NP under efficient reductions. _Role:_ Target of the paper's computational consequence.
- **BQP** — Problems efficiently solvable by bounded-error quantum computation. _Role:_ Standard linear quantum computation baseline.
- **Nonlinear quantum computation** — Quantum evolution where dynamics depend on the quantum state itself. _Role:_ Mechanism that can change computational power.

## Framework comparison

- **Classical computation** — typical class: P; efficient NP-complete power: No known mechanism; role: Baseline.
- **Linear quantum computation** — typical class: BQP; efficient NP-complete power: Not expected generically; role: Standard quantum baseline.
- **Nonlinear quantum dynamics** — typical class: Beyond standard BQP; efficient NP-complete power: Possible in generic nonlinear models; role: Computational warning signal.
- **Semiclassical gravity** — typical class: Classical gravity + quantum matter; efficient NP-complete power: Paper argues yes under its assumptions; role: Theory under test.

## PECTT

- **Statement:** No physically realizable process efficiently solves NP-complete problems.
- **Role:** Constrains which physical theories are plausible if their dynamics imply computational power beyond observed systems.

## Engineering statement

- **Statement:** Physical theories should preserve observed computational constraints.
- **Observation:** Semiclassical gravity introduces nonlinear quantum dynamics.
- **Context:** Generic nonlinear quantum theories can efficiently solve NP-complete problems.
- **Next Step:** Determine experimentally whether gravity is fundamentally quantum or requires a different coupling mechanism.

## Bottom line

The significance of arXiv:2606.14806 is not that gravity solves NP-complete problems. The significance is that computational complexity may constrain which physical theories remain plausible. If a theory generically permits efficient NP-complete computation, then observed computational limits become evidence relevant to that theory.
