## Core assumptions

- **Gravity is classical** — Keeps the gravitational field outside quantum dynamics.
- **Matter is quantum** — Quantum states source the gravitational field.
- **Coupling is semiclassical** — Classical gravity responds to quantum expectation values.
- **Weak-field limit applies** — Connects semiclassical Einstein dynamics to Schrödinger–Newton dynamics.
- **Physical computation obeys observed limits** — PECTT rules out efficient physical solutions to NP-complete problems.

## Key terms

- **P** — Problems efficiently solvable by deterministic classical computation.
- **BQP** — Problems efficiently solvable by bounded-error quantum computation.
- **NP** — Problems whose proposed solutions can be efficiently verified.
- **NP-complete** — Hardest problems in NP under efficient reductions.
- **PECTT** — Physical Extended Church–Turing Thesis: physically realizable processes do not efficiently solve NP-complete problems.
- **Schrödinger–Newton equation** — Weak-field semiclassical dynamics where a quantum state sources a classical gravitational potential.
- **Nonlinear quantum dynamics** — Dynamics where evolution depends on the quantum state itself.

## Argument map

1. Semiclassical gravity couples quantum matter to classical gravity.
2. In the weak-field limit, this leads toward Schrödinger–Newton-type dynamics.
3. These dynamics are nonlinear.
4. Nonlinear quantum dynamics can enable efficient solutions to NP-complete problems.
5. Observed physical computation appears constrained by PECTT.
6. Therefore, computational complexity constrains which gravity theories remain plausible.
