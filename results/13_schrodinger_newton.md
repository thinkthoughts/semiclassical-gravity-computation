# 13 Schrödinger–Newton Dynamics

## Purpose

This notebook follows the route from semiclassical gravity toward Schrödinger–Newton-type dynamics. The goal is to identify where nonlinear state-dependence enters the argument.

## Semiclassical pathway

1. **Semiclassical Einstein equation** — Classical spacetime curvature responds to quantum matter expectation values.
2. **Weak-field limit** — The relativistic gravitational field is approximated by a Newtonian potential.
3. **Newtonian gravitational potential** — The potential is determined by a mass density source.
4. **Quantum mass density** — The source depends on the quantum state.
5. **Schrödinger–Newton dynamics** — The quantum state evolves under a potential generated by that same state.
6. **Nonlinear state-dependent evolution** — The evolution depends on the state being evolved.

## Equation-level sketch

- **Semiclassical Einstein equation** — `G = expectation value of T`. Couples classical gravity to quantum matter.
- **Poisson equation** — `∇²Φ = source density`. Weak-field Newtonian gravitational potential.
- **Quantum source** — `source density depends on |ψ|²`. The quantum state sources the potential.
- **Schrödinger–Newton equation** — `i∂ψ/∂t = H[ψ]ψ`. The Hamiltonian depends on the state.

## Where the nonlinearity enters

- **Linear quantum mechanics** — evolution: `i∂ψ/∂t = Hψ`; state-dependence: H is fixed externally; computational relevance: Standard BQP-style baseline.
- **Schrödinger–Newton-type dynamics** — evolution: `i∂ψ/∂t = H[ψ]ψ`; state-dependence: H depends on ψ through the gravitational potential; computational relevance: Nonlinearity can change computational power.

## Bottom line

The Schrödinger–Newton equation matters here because the gravitational potential depends on the quantum state. That state-dependence makes the evolution nonlinear. The next notebook studies why nonlinear quantum dynamics can change computational power.
